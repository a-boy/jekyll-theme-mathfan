## Euler's Identity

One of the most beautiful equations in mathematics:

$$e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$$

This equation relates five fundamental mathematical constants:
- $e$ (Euler's number)
- $i$ (imaginary unit)
- $\pi$ (pi)
- $1$ (multiplicative identity)
- $0$ (additive identity)

## Significance

Euler's identity is a special case of Euler's formula:

$$e^{ix} = \cos(x) + i\sin(x)$$

When $x = \pi$, we get:
$$e^{i\pi} = \cos(\pi) + i\sin(\pi) = -1 + 0 = -1$$

Therefore:
$$e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$$
